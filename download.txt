Baseline test
Module 2: The Partnership Foundation (Trust & Status)
Concept-Based MCQs
1. According to the SCARF model, a veteran teacher saying they don’t need a coach is a direct threat to:
   * A) Certainty
   * B) Status
   * C) Autonomy
   * D) Relatedness
2. When a teacher displays "Flight" behavior (minimal responses), it likely indicates the coach has:
   * A) Failed to provide enough expert advice.
   * B) Triggered a Status Threat by using evaluative language rather than low-inference data.
   * C) Spent too much time listening.
   * D) Not followed the NEO-1 checklist strictly enough.
3. A Principal demands the individual engagement scores of all teachers to decide on "Show Cause" notices. According to the Universal SOP, you should:
   * A) Share the scores but ask the Principal to keep them confidential.
   * B) Provide a list of only the "top-performing" teachers.
   * C) Refuse and offer a "System-Trends" report to protect individual trust.
   * D) Tell the Principal you will ask the teachers for permission first.
Situation-Based MCQs (Case Studies)
4. Case Study: A veteran teacher reacts with "Freeze" behavior (passive compliance). Which Opening Script best uses Equality and Voice to establish a partnership?
   * A) "I’m here to help you improve your classroom management with some tips."
   * B) "I’m here as a partner to learn alongside you; what is a specific goal you have for your students today that we can look at together?"
   * C) "The District Office requires me to audit this lesson for performance tracking."
   * D) "I will be watching to see if you are following the standard manual correctly."
5. Case Study: During a feedback session, a teacher is defensive. To move to a Side-by-Side mindset, you should:
   * A) Re-read the rubric to show them exactly where they failed.
   * B) Physically sit next to them and look at student work together, asking "What do you see here?"
   * C) Remind them that your role is to give expert advice they must follow.
   * D) Suggest they observe a younger teacher who is more compliant.
6. Case Study: You notice a teacher is struggling with a noisy class. Instead of giving a "fix," you use Deep Empathy by saying:
   * A) "You should use a whistle to get their attention."
   * B) "It sounds frustrating when you've planned a lesson and the back row isn't engaging. What have you noticed about when they do pay attention?"
   * C) "In my day, I handled 80 students by doing X."
   * D) "I will mark this as a practice visit so it doesn't hurt your record."

Module 3: The Mirror Specialist (Shared Reality)
Concept-Based MCQs
1. What is the primary purpose of capturing "Data at the Edge" (e.g., back-row notebooks)?
   * A) To catch the teacher ignoring students.
   * B) To find the truth of student learning that is often hidden by teacher activity at the "Center."
   * C) To provide evidence for a "Show Cause" notice.
   * D) To satisfy digital app requirements.
2. If a coach and teacher score the same lesson differently, this "Calibration Gap" is usually caused by:
   * A) One person being a "mean" grader.
   * B) Using subjective "feelings" instead of a shared mirror of objective facts.
   * C) The rubric being too complex to understand.
   * D) The teacher acting differently toward each of you.
3. The Human Filter rule states that a coach should NOT capture an artifact if:
   * A) The lighting in the room is poor.
   * B) A student is visibly distressed or the teacher is in an acute emotional crisis.
   * C) The coach forgot their tablet.
   * D) The teacher is using a non-standard strategy.
Situation-Based MCQs (Case Studies)
4. Case Study: Which observation note successfully passes the "Camera Test" by removing high-inference judgment?
   * A) "The teacher was too lazy to check homework."
   * B) "At 11:15 AM, 12 of 68 students were writing in notebooks; 56 students sat with blank pages."
   * C) "The teacher gave a very clear explanation of the topic."
   * D) "The classroom was noisy because the teacher lost control."
5. Case Study: A teacher insists a class was "perfect," but data shows 0% passed the exit ticket. To achieve Calibration, you should:**
   * A) Argue until they admit they were wrong.
   * B) Introduce the "Third Partner" by looking at 5 randomly selected student notebooks together.
   * C) Agree with them to maintain the relationship and try again next week.
   * D) Inform the Principal the teacher is in denial.
6. Case Study: When taking a digital photo of student work, the Voice principle requires you to:
   * A) Take it silently to avoid distracting the class.
   * B) Use a permission script that names a specific learning curiosity (e.g., "I'm curious how they solved this").
   * C) Only take photos of top-performing students.
   * D) Send the photo to the Principal immediately for validation.

Module 4: Digital & Data Intelligence (Collaborative Analytics)
Concept-Based MCQs
1. Coach Usman had 6 visits. 1 holiday, 1 absent teacher (excluded), 1 visit with no artifact, and 1 interrupted by a Principal. What is his WRER?
   * A) 66%
   * B) 50% (Missing artifact and interruption count as 0% for those visits).
   * C) 75%
   * D) 40%
2. What does a "High Fidelity" but "Low Impact" score on a Regional Heatmap suggest?
   * A) Teachers are following steps (Compliance) but without deep pedagogical dialogue (Praxis).
   * B) The app is not being used enough.
   * C) Students are not participating.
   * D) The coach is not visiting enough.
3. To avoid the "Administrative After-Burn," a coach should:
   * A) Take paper notes and enter them at home.
   * B) Complete 100% of app entries (Evidence and Action Steps) inside the school building.
   * C) Ask the teacher to enter data.
   * D) Only record successful visits.
Situation-Based MCQs (Case Studies)
4. Case Study: A Principal displaces your coaching block with "Protocol Duty." Which Advocacy Script best protects your time?
   * A) "I’m sorry, but I have too much work to do today."
   * B) "My WRER is currently at 50%; if I miss this block, Teacher Sara will wait another 7 days for feedback, risking student engagement."
   * C) "I will do the duty if you promise to give me extra time tomorrow."
   * D) "The District Office says I am not allowed to do protocol duty."
5. Case Study: An AI dashboard suggests "Use digital tools," but there is no electricity. Following Human Override, you:
   * A) Tell the teacher to follow the AI suggestion anyway.
   * B) Co-design a low-tech alternative (e.g., "Turn-and-Talk") that achieves the same intent.
   * C) Mark the visit as "Not Applicable."
   * D) Report the lack of resources and skip the coaching step.
6. Case Study: A dashboard shows 100% task completion, but you observe students just copying from the board. You should:
   * A) Ignore the observation and celebrate the 100% score.
   * B) Use the "Shared Mirror" to ask: "Data shows 100% completion, but looking at these notebooks, what do we notice about actual reasoning?"
   * C) Change the dashboard score manually to 0%.
   * D) Report the teacher for "Robotic Teaching."

Module 5: The Instructional Catalyst (Co-Design)
Concept-Based MCQs
1. A teacher explains a strategy perfectly but fails to use it in a noisy classroom. This is a:
   * A) Planning Loop failure
   * B) Observation Loop failure
   * C) Training Loop failure (Needs rehearsal to build muscle memory).
   * D) Mindset failure
2. In Side-by-Side Co-Modeling, the coach’s goal is to:
   * A) Show the teacher they are the expert.
   * B) Act as a "Co-Pilot" by "sliding in" for 2 minutes to model a specific micro-skill.
   * C) Finish the lesson for the teacher.
   * D) Evaluate students.
3. If a goal is not met after two visits, the Improve Phase requires one of 4 Paths. Which is NOT a path?
   * A) Modify the strategy
   * B) Switch to a new strategy
   * C) Stay the course
   * D) Report failure to administration
Situation-Based MCQs (Case Studies)
4. Case Study: A teacher has students copy an entire textbook chapter. You identify the Belief Gap (Internal Rule) as:
   * A) The teacher doesn't know the subject matter.
   * B) The "Silence Myth": The teacher believes a quiet class copying text is a learning class.
   * C) The teacher is lazy and doesn't want to teach.
   * D) The students are too slow to do any other activity.
5. Case Study: A teacher spends 20 minutes on a 5-minute intro. You diagnose this as a Planning Loop failure and:
   * A) Tell them to be faster next time.
   * B) Co-design a script with specific time-stamps for each lesson segment.
   * C) Model the entire lesson for them.
   * D) Mark them as "Not Proficient" in time management.
6. Case Study: When a teacher has 8 skill gaps, a "Catalyst" coach prioritizes:**
   * A) The easiest gap to fix.
   * B) The "High-Leverage" change that the teacher agrees will impact students most.
   * C) The gap the Principal is most concerned about.
   * D) All 8 gaps simultaneously to ensure rapid growth.

Module 6: The Excellence Loop (Reciprocity & Praxis)
Concept-Based MCQs
1. "Responsive Contextualization" is necessary when:
   * A) The teacher is unwilling to follow the manual.
   * B) A strategy is impossible due to local constraints (60 students, bolted desks).
   * C) The coach wants to try a new experiment.
   * D) The Principal demands a change.
2. The "Compliance Trap" occurs when:
   * A) WRER is 0% but growth is high.
   * B) WRER is 100% (visits happening) but Growth Rate is 0% (no behavior change).
   * C) Teacher refuses to sign notes.
   * D) Principal takes over coaching.
3. "Closing the Loop" is only achieved when:
   * A) The report is filed.
   * B) The coach gives a compliment.
   * C) Coach and teacher verify together (Reciprocity) that the new skill is a mastered habit.
   * D) Training is completed.
Situation-Based MCQs (Case Studies)
4. Case Study: A veteran teacher is skeptical of a new strategy. The most Reciprocal move is to:
   * A) Remind them this is the new "Gold Standard."
   * B) Acknowledge their expertise and ask which part of the strategy will work for their community.
   * C) Suggest they observe a younger teacher.
   * D) Perform modeling without asking permission.
5. Case Study: You model a strategy and it fails (chaotic classroom). To maintain Shared Reality, you:**
   * A) Blame previous student behavior.
   * B) Use the "Shared Mirror" to admit failure and ask: "What did you notice that I missed?"
   * C) Pretend it went well to maintain your "Expert" status.
   * D) Delete the failure recording from the app.
6. Case Study: Why is Praxis (action-based learning) prioritized over "Abstract Theory"?
   * A) Theory is too difficult to read.
   * B) It allows the "Human Filter" to adapt the intent of a strategy to fit local high-constraint reality.
   * C) It is easier for the coach to grade.
   * D) The manual is only a suggestion and not important.
